Radio

Extended Technique

“Extended Technique” is my weekly three-hour radio show on WKCR, on Wednesdays between 3-6pm. The show is by the definition given by WKCR is originally intended for avant-garde classical, but I use it as a space to showcase full film scores. As a former figure skater, I was introduced to the world of film music through the soundtracks I competed to, and heard other people practice and compete to. In college, I studied film and paid close attention to how music shapes cinematic emotion. With a three hour block, this show lets me explore that — I introduce each composer and film, share information I have learned, and let the full score play out, listening to the themes evolve across the arc of a film.

Friday Afternoon Classical and Cereal Music

Friday Afternoon Classical was my weekly WKCR show in Spring 2023, every Friday from 3 to 6pm, with a dedicated Bach Hour from 5 to 6. I’ve always loved classical music — I grew up playing violin and doing ballet (and still dance now), so it’s been a big part of my life. This show was pretty freeform; I’d decide what to play based on what I found in the station’s library that day. I don’t have a big archive from it, but it was a fun space to explore and share whatever I was excited about in the moment. Cereal Music had a similar vibe. I hosted it every Tuesday in Spring 2025 from 9:30am to noon. Similar to Friday Afternoon Classical but in the mornin, it was a calm way to start the day with whatever I felt like playing.
